Reality Brief


About

Nationalism, sensationalism and the propaganda model cause many people to consume a dangerously biased assortment of news. Using the miracles of the internet, Reality Brief reduces a degree of bias by automatically reporting every instance in the world of a variety of predetermined types of events.

More specifically, Reality Brief uses the Wikimedia API to pull current information from crowdsourced lists. It also uses the Sigimera API to pull natural disaster data from gdacs.org.

Automatically included events:

  • Terrorist attacks
  • New heads of state
  • Natural disasters
  • Deaths of public figures
  • Coups
  • Attempted and successful political assasinations
  • Train crashes, airplane crashes and shipwrecks
  • Massacres
  • Major structural failures
  • Major data breaches
  • Riots
  • Wikimedia news items listed under "Politics and Elections", "Conflicts and Attacks" and "International Relations." These headline lists are selected subjectively, but are included because they are fact-based, tend not to be biased toward any one nation, and provide coverage of important events frequently not found in other lists
